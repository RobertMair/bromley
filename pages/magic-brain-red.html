Addiator Summary
Maker: Unkown
Origin: Hong Kong / Japan, c.1950-1960
Description

This magic brain calculator is made up of a red plastic rectangular frame, with an aluminium cover of the mechanism. The centre of the calculator features seven vertical tracks, with numeral "1:9" running along side. The tracks contain a set of red aluminium teeth, which can be adjusted up and down using the stylus. Above the tracks are eight small squares displaying the numerals which are used in the mathematic equations. The top of the device has a wire bar, that when pulled out, resets all the panels to zero, while the right hand side features two small red clips for housing the stylus pen. A large addition, subtraction and multiplication symbol is printed at the bottom. The stylus pen for the magic brain calculator is a thin, cylindrical, metal rod, with flat top at one end and a pointed tip at the other. The pen is inserted into the tracks and can be moved up or down to alter the numerals featured in the windows above.

Other Information

The instruction sheet is a small two sided, printed, document featuring basic information of how to add, subtract, multiply, divide using the magic brain calcullator. There is also a small section on how to reset the device.
