Addiator Summary
Maker: Addiator Gesellschaft
Origin: Germany, c.1950-1970
Description

The addiator calculator is made up of a brass frame, with an aluminium handle at the top.

It is a duplex model, used for addition functions on the obverse and subtraction on the reverse. The main section in the centre consists of eight vertical tracks with numeral "0:9" running along side. Each tracks contains a set of aluminium teeth, which can be adjusted up and down using a stylus. Above the tracks are nine small windows displaying the numerals which are used in the mathematic equations. The top of the device has an aluminium bar, which when pulled out, resets all the panels to zero, while the right hand side features a metal clip, for housing the stylus pen. A plus symbol is printed at the bottom along with the text "ADDITION", while a minus symbol and the word "SUBTRACTION" can be found on the reverse.

The stylus pen for the addiator is a small brass pen, featuring a columned pattern around the outside, leading to a sharp pointed tip. The stylus can be inserted into the various tracks to adjust the numerals found in the windows.

The addiator is housed in a small, olive green, leather pouch which opens at the side. "ADDIATOR" is stamped in bold letters on the front.

Other Information

Accompanying the calculator is a plain opened envelope, addressed to "Dr/Mr Allan G Bromley / University of Sydney", and contains a personal letter from Dan Gartner, regarding the gift of the calculator to Bromley. The letter, typed on an a4 page, details the original provenance of the addiator before it came into Allan's possession.
