Addiator Summary
Maker: Continentale Buroreform (CBR)
Origin: Germany, c.1920-1930
Description

The Correntator addiator is a single sided device, made from a gold plated aluminium, which can be used for both addition and subtraction. It features 10 vertical rows of teeth, with numerals 0:9, 0:1, 0:11, and 0:3/4, as well as ten windows above for viewing the numerals used in the mathematic equations. A flip panel in the centre can be used to switch between addition and subtraction functions. At the top of the device there is a metal bar, which can be pulled out to reset the windows to zero. 'CORRENTATOR' is printed at the top above the windows, with 'PATENT' at the bottom.

Other Information

The case for the correntator is made from plain black leather and opens out like a book. There is an paper sheet attached to the interior of the case featuring instructions on addition, subtraction, zero calculations and resetting the device. Some metal edges for housing the addiator can be found on the opposite interior side.
