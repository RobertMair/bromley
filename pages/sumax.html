Addiator Summary
Maker: Addi GmbH, Klee & Behrens
Origin: Germany, 1920
Description

The Summax addiator style calculator is housed in a notepad style, green leatherette case, featuring 'SUMMAX', embossed on the cover.On the interior of the cover, four slits or tabs are present for housing loose paper, helpful in making additional notes. The addiator is a single sided device, made from a gold plated aluminium, which can be used for both addition and subtraction. It features 10 vertical rows of teeth, with numerals 0:9, 0:1, 0:11, as well as ten windows below for viewing the numerals used in the mathematic equations. A sliding panel in the centre, with a mirror image of the teeth can be used to switch between addition and subtraction functions. At the top of the device there is a single metal rod or bar, which can be pulled pulled out to reset the windows to zero. "SUMMAX / PATENTED" is printed at the top above the teeth.

Other Information

The stylus for the Summax is made from an unpolished timber. It has a flat top and a pointed metal nib.

The notepad style case comes with a collection of four blank pieces of paper, which can be used for making additional calculations. The paper is slightly discoloured and worn.
